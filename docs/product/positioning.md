# Talkie Positioning Notes

## Current read

Talkie is a multi-modal capture tool for knowledge workers.

The core job is simple: help people externalize thoughts quickly, in whatever form is fastest, without breaking flow.

One shorthand that feels useful:

> Talkie is like a selfie for your thoughts.

It captures an idea close to the moment it happened, with enough surrounding context to make it useful later.

## Core value proposition

Talkie should feel like one capture system with multiple entry points:

- voice memos for longer recordings, meetings, and brainstorms
- dictation for fast voice-to-text capture
- notes for typed thoughts
- scratchpad for quick, low-stakes jots
- transcripts for making audio searchable and reusable
- readouts for listening back to captured or transformed content

The product promise is not just "record audio" or "dictate anywhere." It is:

> capture the thought in the cheapest possible way now, then recover, search, transform, and act on it later.

## Differentiators

### 1. Context-aware capture

Talkie understands the environment around a capture:

- source app
- window title
- browser context when available
- device and capture surface

That context makes retrieval and workflow routing smarter.

### 2. Keyboard-first, power-user native

Talkie is for people who live inside tools and switch contexts quickly. The product should feel:

- shortcut-friendly
- search-first
- low-friction
- always available

### 3. Desktop-native, not browser-native

Talkie belongs close to the operating system:

- global capture
- menu bar / utility behavior
- app-aware context
- screenshots, windows, terminals, and workflow surfaces

### 4. AI as an amplifier, not the product

AI makes captured thoughts more useful:

- transcription
- summarization
- search
- workflow routing
- structured outputs

But the product starts with capture and memory, not with prompting.

### 5. Local-first trust

Talkie should consistently read as:

- your thoughts stay yours
- your context is not a cloud analytics feed
- the system is personal infrastructure, not a SaaS inbox

## Messaging guidance

Prefer language like:

- capture in whatever mode is fastest
- externalize thoughts without breaking flow
- pick it up later with context intact
- one system for memos, dictation, notes, and workflows
- a personal memory layer for active work
- a selfie for your thoughts

Avoid reducing Talkie to only one slice, like:

- "voice memo app"
- "dictation app"
- "workflow tool"

Those are all true, but none of them are the whole product.

## Open questions

- "Readouts" likely means playback / listen-back / spoken output, but we should confirm the exact product framing.
- We should decide when to say "voice-first" versus "multi-modal." Internally, multi-modal feels more accurate. Externally, voice-first may still be the simpler entry point.
