# tape-spool

Two stylized cassette tape reels (concentric rings with spoke details) positioned behind the landmark T. Left reel larger (supply), right reel smaller (take-up). Cream T overlaid prominently. Hot Mic dot above.

**What it evokes.** Audio heritage. The name "Talkie" implies voice recording, and the cassette reels ground that in a specific era — magnetic tape, reel-to-reel, the physical act of capturing sound. The T sitting over the reels says: this is a recording tool with lineage. Not retro-kitsch — the reels are subtle, geometric, recessive.

**Where it shines / where it fails.** The most narrative-rich direction. At 1024 the reel detail adds depth without competing with the T. At 40px the reels disappear and it reads as flat-t — which means it degrades gracefully rather than breaking. Works well across all three destinations. The risk is that the reel motif reads as "music" rather than "voice," but the T anchors it to the wordmark.
