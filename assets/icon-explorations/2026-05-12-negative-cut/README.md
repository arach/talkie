# negative-cut

Cream rounded rectangle with the landmark T shape cut out, revealing the canvas beneath. Hot Mic dot sits as solid orange within the cream field. Strong figure-ground reversal.

**What it evokes.** A stamp, a stencil, a brand mark punched through material. The negative space creates presence through absence — the T is defined by what's removed, not added. The cream block reads as a card or a tag, and the orange dot as a seal. There's something official about it.

**Where it shines / where it fails.** The strongest small-size performer in the set. At 40px the cream block is unmistakable and the dark T cutout reads clearly even at Watch sizes. The figure-ground contrast works on any background. On iOS the rounded mask doubles down on the rounded rectangle — the shape-within-a-shape feels intentional. On macOS squircle, the inner rounded rect nests naturally. Only weakness: at 1024 it can feel slightly static — less dynamic than the forward-lean conveys in other treatments.
